Poem: "|Bridles of a Love-Ruined Love"

At dawn, truly wet in gently sickred melodies on the black wire,

binded with silk of leather, and the rain|

doesn't harm my skin with its soul-doomed water.

I spent noon, and he with the clothing of night.

Two more picked flowers for my bouquet| of ruined love.

No need to wallow it soon.

Press forward to the last and bring whole life to it.

The details of that last evening I walk
